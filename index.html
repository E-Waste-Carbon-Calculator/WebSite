Welcome to the E-waste Carbon Calculator, a specialized tool designed to deliver precise and localized assessments of greenhouse gas emissions linked to waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE or e-waste) in Hong Kong. This Calculator aim to empower producers, retailers, and consumers of Regulated Electrical and Electronic Equipment (REE) to gain a deeper understanding of their environmental impact and promote sustainable practices in e- waste management.
Understanding E-waste in Hong Kong
  • What is E-waste?
    Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electrical and electronic equipment that is no longer useful to its owners. In general, the terms “WEEE” and “e-waste” are used synonymously in accordance with the EU WEEE Directive.
  • Where Does All This E-waste go?
    • Export
      For years, much of Hong Kong’s e-waste was exported to developing countries for recycling or reuse. However, stricter international regulations like the Basel Convention have limited the global trade of hazardous waste, forcing Hong Kong to take responsibility for its e-waste locally.
    • Hong Kong E-waste Management
      To address this challenge, Hong Kong introduced the Producer Responsibility Scheme on Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WPRS). To ensure proper recycling and disposal of e-waste, the government has implemented a licensing scheme for e-waste recyclers and established WEEE · PARK to provide free collection and treatment services for Hong Kong residents.
Producer Responsibility Scheme on Waste Electrical
and Electronic Equipment (WPRS)
WEEE · PARK
The WPRS regulates the disposal of key appliances, including air conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, televisions, computers, printers, scanners, and monitors. Effective from 1 July 2024, the scheme has been expanded to cover larger refrigerators, washing machines with higher capacities, tumble dryers, and dehumidifiers, ensuring better oversight of e-waste management and expanding WEEE · PARK’s capacity to collect and recycle a broader range of WEEE.
  • E-Waste Contributes to Climate Change
    Every piece of Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) inevitably generates greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions throughout its lifecycle, from production to disposal. The method of e-waste disposal plays a crucial role in determining the level of these emissions. Improper disposal, especially in landfills, can result in the release of significant amounts of GHGs.
